About ChlamBase

ChlamBase.org, a fork of WikiGenomes.org, is an open source, user-curated database of functional annotations for 4 model Chlamydia species: C. trachomatis 434/BU ( LGV L2 ), C. trachomatis D/UW-3/CX, C. muridarum Nigg, and C. pneumoniae CWL029. A primary goal of this portal is to provide a powerful user-oriented experience tailored to the research interests of the Chlamydia research community, and to the unique biology of Chlamydia. A central feature of ChlamBase is to allow users to view, add, and edit evidence-based gene annotations, engineered mutant strains, orthologous gene comparisons, developmental gene expression, protein interactions, functions, processes, and more. Furthermore, ChlamBase allows visualization of the organism genome using JBrowse at the top of the main page, and also provides a widget for sequence alignment of either dna or amino acids of chlamydia orthologs.

WikiGenomes

WikiGenomes is a portal to the biological content in the Wikidata project. It currently hosts the genes, proteins, and a variety of functional annotations for 120 bacteria genomes, as well as several species of eukaryote. WikiGenomes is designed to be both a resource for basic researchers to browse the collective knowledge of a variety of well studied organisms, and a technology layer that allows the user to add to that collective knowledge. To support this, WikiGenomes provides defined editing forms that prompt the user to provide key information and references. WikiGenomes then adds that knowledge to the Wikidata knowledge graph of genes, diseases, and drugs.

FAQ

1. I want to get a list of all chlamydia genes that have annotated cellular components.
Use the "advanced search" option. Click the "advanced search" dropdown and select the box next to "cellular component" in the first column. Then click the search button.

2. I want to get a list of all chlamydia genes that have the cellular component "pathogen-containing vacuole membrane".
Use the "advanced search" option. Click the "advanced search" dropdown and select the box next to "cellular component" in the first column. Then, type in "pathogen-containing vacuole membrane" next to the checkbox. A list of all matching cellular components will appear, and select the best match. Now, click the search button.

3. I want a list of chlamydia proteins that have molecular function "SH2 domain binding" AND localized to the "extracellular region".
Use the "advanced search" option. Click the "advanced search" dropdown and select the box next to "molecular function" in the first column. Then, type in "sh2" next to the checkbox. "SH2 domain binding" will appear from the dropdown, and select the best match. Click the checkbox next to cellular component and type "extracellular". A dropdown will appear with "extracellular region". Select it, and now, click the search button.

4. How do I get to the advanced search page?
There are several ways. If you are on the landing page, you can do a direct advanced search by using the search bar at the top of the page. If you are on any other page, there is a search bar in the upper right corner. You can type in a keyword you are looking for, or simply leave it blank and click the search button. It will redirect you to the advanced search page.

5. How do I get to the gene page for the chlamydia gene with locus tag CTL0260?
The easiest way is to use the search bar. Start typing CTL0260 and a dropdown will appear. Click CTL0260 and you will be redirected to its page. The other way is to use the browsing page. From the landing page, click the "C. trachomatis 434/BU" node. From there, you will have a list of all genes in that strain. You can use the arrow buttons to find CTL0260.

6. I discovered that CTL0260 localizes to the pathogen-containing (inclusion) membrane. Now what?
Navigate to the gene page for CTL0260 (See #5). Scroll down the page until you see the "Localizations" module. Under the Cellular Component section, click "Add Annotation". You will be guided through the annotation process at this point.

7. How do I authorize my account to use ChlamBase?
Authorization is only required on gene specific pages. That means you will only see the authorization button appear if you are on the gene page describing 'CTL0260'. Once on that page, click the green button in the upper right corner. If you are a new user, make sure to click "Create Account" from the form. Otherwise, skip below to click authorize ChlamBase. Make sure you login with your MediaWiki details. Once you return to ChlamBase after logging in, notice that the login button will turn orange, and in the URL browser you see "authorized". You can now add annotations from ChlamBase!

8. It looks like I'm authorized but I still am not. What do I do now?
Clear your cache. Then, navigate to WikiData and in the upper right corner, click "log in". Then go back to ChlamBase. If you are authorized, deauthorize. Then, reauthorize on ChlamBase and try to add your annotation again.

9. What is MediaWiki? What is WikiData?
ChlamBase stores all of your annotations in an online database called WikiData. WikiData is an open, editable, user-curated knowledge graph of all kinds of information, not just Chlamydia! WikiData is built using MediaWiki (like wikipedia), and ChlamBase is the user interface that sits on top of the WikiData layer that allows easy access to read and write information for non coders.
